Figure 1A. Geographic distribution of confirmed measles cases by state in the United States,
January through May 2026. Colour intensity reflects cumulative laboratory-confirmed case counts
aggregated from county- and district-level records. Thirty-seven states reported at least one
confirmed case, with the heaviest burden concentrated in South Carolina, Utah, Texas, and Florida.
Alaska and Hawaii are not shown on the main map; Hawaii reported one confirmed case during this
period. Source: Johns Hopkins CSSEGISandData measles_county_all_updates.csv; US Census Bureau
TIGER/Line state boundaries (2022).

---

Figure 1B. Top ten states by cumulative laboratory-confirmed measles cases in the United States,
January through 8 May 2026. Bars are sorted from highest to lowest. South Carolina and Utah
together accounted for approximately 60% of all county-attributed cases during this period.
Source: Johns Hopkins CSSEGISandData measles_county_all_updates.csv.

---

Figure 1C. Weekly trajectories of laboratory-confirmed measles cases in the six highest-burden
states, January through 9 May 2026. Each line represents one state, ranked in the legend by
total 2026 case count (n). A pronounced epidemic peak in South Carolina occurred during the
week of 8 March 2026; Utah sustained elevated weekly counts throughout March–April 2026.
Source: Johns Hopkins CSSEGISandData Top_states_time_series.csv.

Figure 1D. National kindergarten MMR coverage in the United States by school year, 2019–20
through 2024–25. The dashed line marks the 95% coverage threshold required for community
immunity against measles. Coverage has fallen continuously since 2019–20 and remains 2.5
percentage points below the target in 2024–25, leaving an estimated 286,000 kindergartners
inadequately protected each year. Source: CDC SchoolVaxView national estimates.
